The 12 Archetypes

Every great question follows a pattern. These are the 12.

🔄
The Reframe
Familiar thing through an unexpected metaphor
"If your job was a crime scene, what's the evidence?"
🎯
Specificity Trap
Precise number that reveals personality
"How many unread texts do you have right now?"
⚖️
False Binary
Two good options — your pick says everything
"Would you rather always be 10 min early or 10 min late?"
🪞
The Mirror
See yourself from the outside
"What would a documentary about your week be called?"
🧪
Thought Experiment
Absurd hypothetical that's secretly revealing
"If you had to teach a class tomorrow, what's the subject?"
🕰️
Time Machine
Memory as a lens on life
"What's the most expensive thing you own that you never use?"
📈
Absurd Escalation
Normal → ridiculous
"What's a $5 thing that genuinely improved your life?"
💔
Vulnerability Door
Gentle emotional opening
"What's a compliment you've never forgotten?"
🏷️
Identity Sort
Self-categorization using unexpected categories
"Are you a Monday person or a Friday person?"
🧠
Explain-It Test
Expose the knowledge-confidence gap
"Explain what you do for work like I'm 5."
🌍
World-Builder
Design your ideal — what's missing reveals what matters
"Design your perfect Sunday. Go."
⛓️
The Chain
Rapid sequence building trust through rhythm
"Favorite color? Season? Sound? Smell? — now WHY those four?"
